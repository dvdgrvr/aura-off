# Visual Identity Spec

## Goal
Give Aura Off a recognizable look without adding complexity.

The art direction should reinforce:
- composure
- presence
- instability
- public success/failure
- social pressure

## Style direction
- minimalist
- silhouette-first
- top-down with 2.5D illusion
- aura is the hero element
- depth comes from shadows, layering, and separation from the ground

## Player look
Player visuals should include:
1. body silhouette
2. shadow
3. ground anchor ring
4. aura ring/glow layers

### Body silhouette
- simple humanoid capsule or rounded-torso shape
- dark fill with subtle rim highlight
- no facial detail
- slight taper to suggest head/torso/stance

### Shadow
- ellipse under player
- gets softer and larger with aura
- remains on ground when player visually “lifts”
- flickers slightly when unstable

### Ground anchor ring
- faint ring under the player’s original ground contact point
- helps sell levitation
- helps preserve positional readability

### Aura
- low aura: faint ring
- mid aura: brighter ring + soft glow
- high aura: layered ring + expansion
- unstable: flicker, distortion, uneven pulse
- release: outward shockwave
- break: inward collapse / pop / fizzle

## State readability
Every player should clearly communicate:

### Neutral
- low shadow separation
- no visible aura or only faint residual glow
- visually quiet

### Building
- slight lift
- stronger ring
- faint pulse
- posture reads more upright

### Dominant
- bigger aura footprint
- clearer separation from shadow
- more space around player
- stronger rim light

### Unstable
- body jitter
- aura flicker/distortion
- shadow pulse becomes uneven
- state should be impossible to miss

### Break
- body compresses/tilts
- aura collapses inward or pops out
- shadow snaps inward
- public-feeling failure

### Release
- clean snap down or controlled grounding
- large shockwave outward
- visually confident and stable

## NPC look
NPCs should be:
- smaller
- simpler
- lower contrast
- more crowd-like than character-like

NPC visuals should include:
- small silhouette body
- tiny directional cue
- simple reaction changes through color/scale/lean

## Depth tricks
Use cheap depth:
- shadow separation
- floor glow
- squashed ellipses
- brighter center area
- subtle vignette
- layer separation

## Things to avoid
- realism
- large sprite sheets
- complex rigging
- detailed clothing
- unnecessary environmental art
